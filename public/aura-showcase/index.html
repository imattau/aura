Nostr-native web

Publish websites
without servers

Aura lets you host complete, multi-page websites on Nostr relays and Blossom storage — identified by your public key, assembled in the browser by a Service Worker, with no DNS and no traditional infrastructure.

How it works View source
Step 1

Nostr manifest

You publish a kind:15128 event mapping your site's URL paths to SHA-256 blob hashes. Your Nostr key is your domain.

Step 2

Blossom storage

Your HTML, CSS, JS and assets live on Blossom servers as immutable, content-addressed blobs — anyone can mirror them.

Step 3

Browser assembles

A Service Worker intercepts navigation requests, fetches the manifest from a relay, retrieves verified blobs, and constructs the page locally.

What makes it different

No DNS

Sites are addressed by Nostr public key — no domain registrar, no nameserver, no ICANN.

No origin server

After the Service Worker installs, zero bytes flow through any server you control.

Content integrity

Every blob is verified by SHA-256 before it reaches the browser. Tampered files are rejected silently.

Censorship resistant

Blobs are public and anyone can mirror them. Relay your manifest to as many relays as you like.

Nostr-native identity

Your npub is your website address. Sign in with NIP-07 or NIP-46 — no passwords.

Versioned publishing

Publish a new manifest event to update your site. Old versions remain accessible if anyone cached them.
